# Infection First Line If Penacillin allergic
1 Meningitis Ceftriaxone IV 2g BD. Add Amox IV 2g QDS if age >55 or immunocompromised. Add acyclovir IV (10mg/kg) if encephalitis suspected. Chloramphenicol IV 25mg/kg QDS. Add Co-trimoxazole 1.44g BD if age >55 or immunocompromised.
2 Community Acquired Pneumonia Mild:Amox PO 1g TDS. Moderate:Doxy PO 200mg OD on day 1, then 100mg OD. Severe:Ben Pen IV 2.4g QDS + Clarithromycin IV 500mg BD Mild:Doxy PO 200mg OD on day 1, then 100mg OD. Moderate:No change. Severe:Vancomycin IV (see guidelines for dosing) + Clarithromycin IV 500mg BD
3 Hospital Acquired Pneumonia and Aspiration Pneumonia Non-Severe:Amox PO/IV 1g TDS + Metronidazole 400mg(if PO) or 500mg(if IV) TDS. Severe:As above + IV Gent (as per guidelines) Co-trimoxazole PO/IV 960mg + Metronidazole 400mg(if PO) or 500mg(if IV) TDS. Severe:As above + IV Gent (as per guidelines)
4 Abdominal Sepsis Amox IV 1g TDS + Metronidazole IV 500mg TDS + Gent Vancomycin + Metronidazole IV 500mg TDS + Gent
5 Uncomplicated UTI Nitrofurantoin PO 50mg QDS (Avoid if eGFR<60)
6 Complicated UTI/Pyelonephritis/Urosepsis Tazocin IV 4.5g TDS for 7 - 10 days Co-trimoxazole IV 960 BD
